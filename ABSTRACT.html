ZaikaTrack

An AI-powered nutrition tracker that finally understands what's actually on an Indian plate.

Abstract

Built for the thali, not the burger bowl

Most calorie-tracking apps are designed around Western food logic — packaged products, fixed portion sizes, barcode scans. They buckle the moment a meal looks like two rotis, a katori of dal, and a small bowl of sabzi. ZaikaTrack is a full-stack web application built specifically around how India eats: a curated desi food database, a fast logging workflow tuned to everyday Indian meals, and a layer of generative AI that can read a free-text meal description and reason about it the way a dietitian familiar with Indian portions would.

The application is built on Next.js with the App Router, backed by Supabase for authentication, Postgres storage, and row-level security, and deployed on Vercel. On top of the core tracking experience — search, log, daily and weekly dashboards with Recharts visualisations — three Gemini-powered features turn the app from a static logbook into something closer to a conversation: a meal analyser that parses natural-language meal descriptions, a weekly diet review that reads back a user's own data as personalised feedback, and a nutrition chatbot fluent in regional Indian cuisine. Built entirely on free-tier infrastructure, ZaikaTrack is a working demonstration that localisation — not just more data — is what makes a nutrition tool genuinely useful.

Problem → Approach

What's missing today

  • Global food databases barely cover regional Indian dishes
  • Portion sizes are listed in grams, not "1 roti" or "1 katori"
  • Logging a mixed home-cooked thali is slow and frustrating
  • No feedback loop that understands desi eating patterns

What ZaikaTrack does

  • ~100 curated Indian dishes across 5 regions & meal types
  • Native serving labels ("1 roti", "1 bowl") alongside grams
  • Free-text meal entry parsed instantly by Gemini AI
  • Weekly AI diet review written against the user's own goal

Key Features

From search bar to full week in review

01

Desi Food Database

~100 dishes across breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks & beverages — searchable by name, tagged by region and category.

02

Auth & Onboarding

Email signup with a guided flow that calculates a personalised calorie goal using the Mifflin-St Jeor formula.

03

Core Tracker

Debounced food search, quantity-aware logging, and a live daily summary grouped by meal type.

04

Weekly Dashboard

Calorie trend lines, macro breakdowns, and meal-distribution charts that turn a week of logs into a story.

05

AI Meal Analyser

Type a meal in plain English — get back Indian-portion-aware calories and macros in seconds.

06

AI Diet Review

A weekly, goal-aware review of what's working and three concrete things to adjust next week.

07

Nutrition Chatbot

Ask "is samosa worse than pakoda?" and get a real answer, grounded in Indian food culture.

Row-Level Security

Every profile and log is locked to its owner at the database layer — not just the UI layer.

Bottom nav, 44px tap targets, and skeleton loaders so the app feels finished on a phone, not just a laptop.

AI Integration · Gemini 1.5 Flash

Three features, one running theme: understand Indian food

5A · TEXT → JSON

Meal Analyser

POST /api/ai/analyse-meal

A user types "2 rotis with dal and a small bowl of sabzi for lunch." Gemini identifies the dishes, maps them to standard Indian portions, and returns structured calories and macros — ready to add straight to the log.

5B · DATA → REVIEW

Weekly Diet Review

POST /api/ai/diet-review

Pulls seven days of logs — average calories, top foods, macro balance — and asks Gemini for a personalised review against the user's stated goal: what's going well, what to fix, three specific next steps.

5C · MULTI-TURN

Nutrition Chatbot

POST /api/ai/chat

A conversational assistant scoped to desi food and regional cuisine — protein in paneer, lighter chaat swaps, what a sensible weight-loss breakfast looks like in an Indian kitchen, rate-limited to stay inside the free tier.

  • managed by Supabase
profiles
  • age, weight_kg, height_cm
  • goal lose · maintain · gain
  • daily_calorie_goal
meal_logs
  • food_id → foods
  • meal_type, quantity
  • calories_consumed
foods (shared)
  • ~100 rows, no owner
  • full nutrient profile
  • region, category
Scope & Known Limitations

Deliberately small, on purpose

Text-only AI inputNo photo-based meal logging — Vision API billing was out of scope for a free-tier college project.
~100 dishes at launchEnough breadth for a convincing demo; the seed script is built to expand later.
Gemini free tier onlyEvery AI feature is designed to degrade gracefully if rate limits are hit mid-demo.
Email auth onlyNo Google or OTP login — kept the auth surface area manageable for one semester.
No meal planningZaikaTrack tracks and reviews; it doesn't plan future meals — explicitly out of scope.
Single-developer buildSolo project across six phases, from scaffolding to a publicly deployed app on Vercel.
